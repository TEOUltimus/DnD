Ironate Reaper

Hit Die

d8.

Requirements

To qualify to become an ironate reaper, a character must fulfill all of the following criteria.

Alignment

Any nongood.

Base Attack Bonus

+4.

Feats

Improved Initiative, Death Blow.

Skills

Heal 4 ranks.

Special

Must have used a coup de grace to kill a creature with at least 10 Hit Dice.

Class Skills

The ironate reaper’s class skills (and the key ability for each skill) are Balance (Dex), Bluff (Cha), Climb (Str), Concentration (Con), Craft (Int), Disable Device (Int), Disguise (Cha) Gather Information (Cha), Heal (Wis), Hide (Dex), Intimidate (Cha), Jump (Str), Listen (Wis), Move Silently (Dex), Open Lock (Dex), Search (Int), Sense Motive (Wis), Sleight of Hand (Dex), Spot (Wis), Swim (Str), Tumble (Dex), Use Magic Device (Cha), and Use Rope (Dex).

Skill Points at Each Level

4 + Int modifier.

Class Features

All of the following are Class Features of the ironate reaper prestige class.

Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Ironate reapers gain proficiency with the light pick, heavy pick, and scythe.

Class Features

When a new even numbered ironate reaper level is gained, the character gains class features and an increase in effective level as if he had also gained a level in a class to which he belonged before adding the prestige class level. The specific class features you gain include spells per day (and spells known, if applicable), improved chance of turning or destroying undead, metamagic or item creation feats, bonus feats, monk special abilities, sneak attack progressions, and so on, depending on the class. You do not, however, gain the benefit of your previous class's Hit Dice, attack progression, skill points, or saving throws. This overlaps (does not stack) with the normal bonus to initiator level from taking levels in a non-initator class. This cannot advance the abilities of a paragon or prestige class beyond their normal end. If you had more than one class before becoming an ironate reaper, you must decide to which class to add each level for the purpose of determining class abilities.

Improved Coup de Grace

Whenever a new ironate reaper level is gained, the character becomes able to deliver a coup de grace to a wider array of enemies.

At 1st level, an ironate reaper is able to deliver a coup de grace to a stunned opponent as though that opponent were helpless. At each subsequent level, the character is able to deliver a coup de grace to opponents with different conditions according to the table above.

At 10th level, an ironate reaper's becomes able to deliver a coup de grace to any opponent just as though that opponent were helpless.

Poison Use

At 2nd level ironate reapers are skilled in the use of poison and never risk accidentally poisoning themselves when applying poison to a blade.

Sudden Strike

As the ninja ability of the same name. The extra damage dealt increases by +1d6 every third level (3rd, 6th, and 9th). If an ironate reaper has a sudden strike bonus from another source, the bonuses on damage stack.

Favored Enemy (Ex)

As the ranger ability of the same name.

An ironate reaper is able to deliver a coup de grace to a Favored Enemy that is helpless (or one of the other conditions covered by his Improved Coup de Grace class feature). He can do this even if that enemy would otherwise be immune to critical hits or precision damage. That enemy loses those immunities for the purpose of the ironate reaper's coup de grace. Even enemies normally immune to effects allowing fortitude saves must make a save against such a blow.

Practiced Coup de Grace

At 5th level, an ironate reaper no longer provokes attacks of opportunity when delivering a coup de grace.

Reaping Blow (Ex)

At 7th level, whenever an ironate reaper scores a critical hit or deals sudden strike damage as part of an attack (not a coup de grace), the target must make a Fortitude save (DC = damage dealt) or die.

Grim Duty (Ex)

At 10th level, an ironate reaper's mastery of meteing out death peaks. He becomes able to substitute a coup de grace for any normal attack such as an attack of opportunity, or for one or more of the attacks when making a full attack.
